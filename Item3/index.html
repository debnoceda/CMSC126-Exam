Mona Lisa

Artwork Info

The Mona Lisa, painted by Leonardo da Vinci between 1503 and 1519, is one of the most famous and enigmatic portraits in art history. It is renowned for the subject's mysterious smile and the detailed sfumato technique that creates a lifelike effect. The painting is housed in the Louvre Museum in Paris and has captivated audiences for centuries.

About the Artist

Leonardo da Vinci (1452–1519) was a Renaissance polymath, excelling as a painter, inventor, scientist, and engineer. His studies of anatomy and light contributed to the lifelike quality of his paintings. In addition to Mona Lisa, he is also known for The Last Supper, Vitruvian Man, and countless scientific sketches that were ahead of his time.

Impression, Sunrise

Artwork Info

Impression, Sunrise (1872) by Claude Monet is the painting that gave the Impressionist movement its name. The artwork depicts the port of Le Havre at dawn, with quick, visible brushstrokes that capture the fleeting effects of light and atmosphere. The painting was criticized at first but later became a celebrated masterpiece of modern art.

About the Artist

Claude Monet (1840–1926) was a French Impressionist painter fascinated by light and color. He often painted the same subject multiple times under different lighting conditions. His famous works include Water Lilies, Haystacks, and Rouen Cathedral Series.

The Kiss

Artwork Info

The Kiss (1907-1908) by Gustav Klimt is a stunning example of the Vienna Secession movement. The painting, covered in gold leaf, depicts a passionate embrace between two lovers wrapped in intricate, decorative robes. It is considered one of the most romantic and iconic artworks in Western art.

About the Artist

Gustav Klimt (1862–1918) was an Austrian Symbolist painter known for his use of gold leaf and elaborate patterns. His works often explore themes of love, sensuality, and mysticism. His other famous paintings include Portrait of Adele Bloch-Bauer I and The Tree of Life.

Wanderer above the Sea of Fog

Artwork Info

Wanderer above the Sea of Fog (1818) by Caspar David Friedrich is a masterpiece of German Romanticism. The painting depicts a man standing on a rocky cliff, looking over a vast, fog-covered landscape. It symbolizes introspection, the power of nature, and human insignificance in the grand scheme of the universe.

About the Artist

Caspar David Friedrich (1774–1840) was a German Romantic painter known for his moody landscapes and dramatic use of light and shadow. His works, such as The Sea of Ice and Abbey in the Oakwood, convey a deep sense of spirituality and emotion, often featuring solitary figures against vast, untamed nature.
